Matrix Representation of Sample Mean and Sample covariance

Data:

X=(x1x2⋯xN)T=(x1Tx2T⋮xNT)=(x11x12⋯x1px21x22⋯x2p⋮⋮⋱⋮xN1xN2⋯xNp)N×pX=\left( \begin{matrix} x_1& x_2& \cdots& x_N\\ \end{matrix} \right) ^T=\left( \begin{array}{c} x_{1}^{T}\\ x_{2}^{T}\\ \vdots\\ x_{N}^{T}\\ \end{array} \right) =\left( \begin{matrix} x_{11}& x_{12}& \cdots& x_{1p}\\ x_{21}& x_{22}& \cdots& x_{2p}\\ \vdots& \vdots& \ddots& \vdots\\ x_{N1}& x_{N2}& \cdots& x_{Np}\\ \end{matrix} \right) _{N\times p} X=(x1​​x2​​⋯​xN​​)T=⎝⎜⎜⎜⎛​x1T​x2T​⋮xNT​​⎠⎟⎟⎟⎞​=⎝⎜⎜⎜⎛​x11​x21​⋮xN1​​x12​x22​⋮xN2​​⋯⋯⋱⋯​x1p​x2p​⋮xNp​​⎠⎟⎟⎟⎞​N×p​

where xi∈Rp,i=1,2,...,Nx_i\in\R^p, i=1,2,...,Nxi​∈Rp,i=1,2,...,N is the samples.

As for the sample mean, we have

xˉ=1N∑i=1Nxi=1N(x1x2⋯xN)(1⋮1)N×1=1NXT1N\bar{x}=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N{x_i}=\frac{1}{N}\left( \begin{matrix} x_1& x_2& \cdots& x_N\\ \end{matrix} \right) \left( \begin{array}{c} 1\\ \vdots\\ 1\\ \end{array} \right) _{N\times 1}=\frac{1}{N}X^T\mathbf{1}_{\text{N}} xˉ=N1​i=1∑N​xi​=N1​(x1​​x2​​⋯​xN​​)⎝⎜⎛​1⋮1​⎠⎟⎞​N×1​=N1​XT1N​

where 1N=(1⋮1)N×1\mathbf{1}_{\text{N}}=\left( \begin{array}{c} 1\\ \vdots\\ 1\\ \end{array} \right) _{N\times 1}1N​=⎝⎜⎛​1⋮1​⎠⎟⎞​N×1​.

Then the sample covariance can be written as

S=1N∑i=1N(xi−xˉ)(xi−xˉ)T=1N(x1−xˉx2−xˉ⋯xN−xˉ)((x1−xˉ)T(x2−xˉ)T⋮(xN−xˉ)T)=1N(XT−xˉ1NT)(XT−xˉ1NT)T=1NXT(IN−1N1N1NT)(IN−1N1N1NT)TX=1NXTHNHNTX=1NXTHX\begin{aligned} S=&\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N{\left( x_i-\bar{x} \right) \left( x_i-\bar{x} \right) ^T} \\ =&\frac{1}{N}\left( \begin{matrix} x_1-\bar{x}& x_2-\bar{x}& \cdots& x_N-\bar{x}\\ \end{matrix} \right) \left( \begin{array}{c} \left( x_1-\bar{x} \right) ^T\\ \left( x_2-\bar{x} \right) ^T\\ \vdots\\ \left( x_N-\bar{x} \right) ^T\\ \end{array} \right) \\ =&\frac{1}{N}\left( X^T-\bar{x}\mathbf{1}_{N}^{T} \right) \left( X^T-\bar{x}\mathbf{1}_{N}^{T} \right) ^T \\ =&\frac{1}{N}X^T\left( I_N-\frac{1}{N}\mathbf{1}_N\mathbf{1}_{N}^{T} \right) \left( I_N-\frac{1}{N}\mathbf{1}_N\mathbf{1}_{N}^{T} \right) ^TX \\ =&\frac{1}{N}X^TH_NH_{N}^{T}X=\frac{1}{N}X^THX \end{aligned} S=====​N1​i=1∑N​(xi​−xˉ)(xi​−xˉ)TN1​(x1​−xˉ​x2​−xˉ​⋯​xN​−xˉ​)⎝⎜⎜⎜⎛​(x1​−xˉ)T(x2​−xˉ)T⋮(xN​−xˉ)T​⎠⎟⎟⎟⎞​N1​(XT−xˉ1NT​)(XT−xˉ1NT​)TN1​XT(IN​−N1​1N​1NT​)(IN​−N1​1N​1NT​)TXN1​XTHN​HNT​X=N1​XTHX​

where H:=HN=IN−1N1N1NTH:=H_N=I_N-\frac{1}{N}\mathbf{1}_N\mathbf{1}_{N}^{T}H:=HN​=IN​−N1​1N​1NT​ is called the centering matrix.

Principal Components Analysis (PCA)

The goal of PCA is to find a unit vector uuu to maximize the variance of the projections of (xi−xˉ)(x_i-\bar{x})(xi​−xˉ)s on uuu That is,

u^1=argmax⁡u  1N∑i=1N((xi−xˉ)Tu)2=argmax⁡u  uT(1N∑i=1N((xi−xˉ)(xi−xˉ)T))u=argmax⁡u  uTSu\begin{aligned} \hat{u}_1=&\underset{u}{\text{arg}\max}\,\,\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N{\left( \left( x_i-\bar{x} \right) ^Tu \right) ^2} \\ =&\underset{u}{\text{arg}\max}\,\,u^{T}\left( \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N{\left( \left( x_i-\bar{x} \right) \left( x_i-\bar{x} \right) ^T \right)} \right) u \\ =&\underset{u}{\text{arg}\max}\,\,u^{T}Su \end{aligned} u^1​===​uargmax​N1​i=1∑N​((xi​−xˉ)Tu)2uargmax​uT(N1​i=1∑N​((xi​−xˉ)(xi​−xˉ)T))uuargmax​uTSu​

Through the Lagrange Multiplier Method, it is easy to obtain Su=λuSu=\lambda uSu=λu. That is, uuus are the eigenvectors of the sample covariance matrix SSS, and λ\lambdaλs are the corresponding eigenvalues.

In order to conduct the dimensionality reduction (dimension p→qp\rightarrow qp→q), we just need to find all the eigenvalues, then pick the qqq eigenvectors corresponding to the largest qqq eigenvalues.
