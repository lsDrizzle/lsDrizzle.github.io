Singular Value Decomposition

Singular Value Decomposition (SVD): Suppose that AAA is an m×nm\times nm×n matrix, then its SVD can be written as

A=UDVTA=UDV^T A=UDVT

where UUU is an m×mm\times mm×m orthogonal matrix, DDD an m×nm\times nm×n orthogonal matrix and VVV an n×nn\times nn×n diagonal matrix. The elements along the diagonal of DDD are known as the singular values of AAA. The columns of UUU are known as the left-singular vectors. The columns of VVV are known as the right-singular vectors.

Every real matrix has an SVD.

The left-singular vectors of AAA are the eigenvectors of AATAA^TAAT. The right-singular vectors of AAA are the eigenvectors of ATAA^TAATA. The nonzero singular values of AAA are the square roots of the eigenvalues of ATAA^TAATA and AATAA^TAAT.
