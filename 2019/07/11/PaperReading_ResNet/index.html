Paper Information

Title: Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition

Authors: Kaiming He, Xiangyu Zhang, Shaoqing Ren, Jian Sun

Achievements: Best paper in CVPR 2016

Content

Background

Degradation Problem

With the network depth increasing, accuracy gets saturated and then degrades rapidly. This is not caused by overfitting. And adding more layers to a suitably deep model leads to higher training error.

Errors on CIFAR-10

Current Solution

Let the added layers be identity mapping. Therefore, a deeper network should produce no higher training error than its shallower counterpart. However, we don’t know how to find deeper networks that are better than the counterparts.

Proposal

Residual Learning

Consider H(x)H(\mathbf{x})H(x) as the ideal underlying mapping that a few stacked layers try to fit, where x\mathbf{x}x is the inputs to the first of these layers. Rather than expect the layers to approximate H(x)H(\mathbf{x})H(x), we let them approximate a residual function F(x)=H(x)−xF(\mathbf{x})=H(\mathbf{x})-\mathbf{x}F(x)=H(x)−x, and add x\mathbf{x}x to the outputs of those layers to obtain H(x)H(\mathbf{x})H(x).

A building block of residual learning.

An important hypothesis, in order to make residual learning easier than traditional structure, is that for the stacked layers, fitting a residual function F(x)F(\mathbf{x})F(x) is easier than fitting the entire function H(x)H(\mathbf{x})H(x).
